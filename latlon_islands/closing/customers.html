The first LatLon notebooks series: Islands

A series of high quality notebooks based on Islands around the world with a beautiful letterpress cover built using geographical open data.

As with our first notebook, the covers will be beautiful letterpressed topographic illustrations of the different islands they represent, printed by Reykjavik LetterPress using the best paper and their long experience.

Additionally, we'll release all the vector work generated in the notebooks as open source, giving early access to project's backers.

Martian Islands

We wanted to make something extra special for all our incredible backers, something most people haven’t even heard about. So we went to the stars… and found the Tear-drop islands on Mars.

There are theories and findings which lead us to believe Mars was once full of liquid water. Where there is water there are rivers and lakes. When water flows, it carves channels and creates islands.

With the help of data collected by NASA and its MOLA program, we've found some islands and we're creating a special green notebook. These islands site south of Chryse Planitia and we'll be using them for the full-bleed design of the extra notebook, creating an amazing cover.
